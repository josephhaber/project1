Our Head Coaches have been stars at every level of the game

  • Kyrie Irving is the 28-year-old starting point guard for the Brooklyn Nets. The son of a professional basketball player, Irving was born in Melbourne Australia, and grew up in West Orange, NJ, where he quickly became touted as one of the nation's top high school prospects. After a year at Duke, the Cleveland Cavaliers drafted Irving first overall in the 2011 NBA draft, and he won a title with the team in 2016. Irving has won numerous NBA awards, including the All-Star Game MVP and NBA Three-Point-Contest Champion.

  • Stephen Curry is a combo guard for the Golden State Warriors and is credited with helping to revolutionize the game of basketball with his incredible outside shooting ability. "Steph," the older son of NBA legend Dell Curry, played college basketball for the Davidson Wildcats before being drafted by the Warriors 7th overall in 2009. He has won two league MVPs, led the Warriors to three NBA championships and is regarded as perhaps the greatest shooter of all time.

Despite crowing up a soccer player and not touching a basketball until the age of 15, the now perennial All-Star has dominated the league since his arrival in 2014 with an array of post-moves rivaling those Hakeem Olajuwon. Embiid has helped to turn around a struggling Sixers franchise and has developed into one of the best defensive bigs in the league, as well.
